# Company Background

</div>

**OceanMist Ltd.** is an Indonesia-based manufacturing and retail company that produces premium body wash and soap products for both domestic (local) and international markets. Established in 2020, the company has grown rapidly amid increasing consumer awareness of skin health and post-pandemic hygiene standards.

Currently, **OceanMist Ltd.**'s book of business is approaching **50,000 customers** and possesses over **150,000 transactions**, generating sales revenue exceeding **IDR 15 billion**. The available data spans various dimensions and metrics, including product category performance (liquid vs. bar soap), production efficiency, and distribution channel effectiveness.

Reporting to the **Head of Operations**, an in-depth analysis was conducted to evaluate OceanMist's performance over the past few years (2022–2024). This comprehensive review provides valuable insights that internal cross-functional teams will utilize to streamline processes and enhance commercial performance.

## North Star Metrics

# 📊 Executive Summary

</div>
**Sales Revenue Trends (2022–2024)**

### 1. Revenue Stability & Overall Range

- Revenue remained **relatively stable** across all three years, consistently ranging between **~$7.9M and ~$9.5M** per month.
- No single year showed dramatic growth or decline, indicating a **mature and steady** sales pattern.
- **October 2024** posted the highest single-month figure at approximately **$9.5M** — the peak across the entire period.

### 2. Year-over-Year Performance

-	2022 showed the highest variability, with notable peaks in January, August, and October ($9.3–9.4M) alongside a sharp February low ($7.9M).
-	2023 was the most consistent year, with most months clustering around $8.2M–$8.7M, reflecting stable but modest performance.
-	2024 showed a gradual upward trend mid-year before a sharp November decline, suggesting either market softening or seasonal effects.

### 3. Quarterly Insights & Seasonal Trends

- **Q1** across all years consistently started with a dip in **February** — the lowest monthly revenue each year ($7.9M in 2022 & 2023, $8.2M in 2024).
- **Q3–Q4** generally showed stronger performance, particularly in **July–August and October**, suggesting seasonal demand peaks.
- **November 2024** saw a notable drop to ~$7.8M — the lowest point in 2024, signaling a potential year-end slowdown.

### 4. Key Takeaways & Recommendations

-	Investigate the recurring February dip each year — targeted promotions or campaigns could help lift this underperforming month.
-	Leverage the strong Q3 and October performance windows to maximize revenue through seasonal marketing pushes.
-	Monitor the November 2024 decline closely — if the trend continues into December, a strategic review of Q4 pricing and promotions is recommended.

# Insight Deep-Dive
# Sales Trend

</div>

**Sales Revenue**
#### 1. Critical Sales Anomaly in Q4 2024
- After reaching an all-time high in **October 2024**, revenue **plummeted in November 2024 (a 20% decline)**.
- November 2024 sales represented the **worst performance in the past three years**.
- This indicates critical issues — such as distribution disruptions or operational stockouts — that require **immediate investigation**.

#### 2. Recurring Seasonal Slump in February
- There is a **recurring seasonal decline every February** (2022, 2023, and 2024).
- Sales consistently experience a sharp decline after January.
- Companies must plan special promotional strategies or adjust inventory early to mitigate this annual decline.

#### 3. General Stability with 2024 Growth Momentum
- Historically (2022–2023), sales trends tended to be stable, with fluctuations remaining within a certain range.
- Prior to the November anomaly, **Q1–Q3 of 2024 showed a stronger trend** compared to same periods in previous years.
- This indicates **positive market demand growth** until the disruption at year-end.

**Average Order Value (AOV)**
#### 1. Historic Peak in December 2023
- Sharp spike in **December 2023** — AOV reached a high of **9.72**.
- Customers tended to purchase **larger quantities** or opt for **higher-priced product bundles** during this period.

#### 2. Consistent Mid-Year Volatility (May–June)
- Fairly extreme fluctuation between **May and June** each year.
- In 2023, there was a surge from the low point in June (8.77), rising significantly again — indicating a **seasonal shift** in purchasing behavior.

#### 3. General Stability with Downward Pressure in Late 2024
- Overall, AOV remained stable within the range of **8.80 to 9.40**.
- Downward trend toward the end of 2024 (Oct–Nov) reinforces that the decline in total revenue was also influenced by **a decrease in average cart value**.

**Order Count**
#### 1. Critical Performance Anomaly in Q4 2024
- After reaching a record high in **October 2024** with over **1,100,000 orders**, the number dropped to around **900,000 in November 2024**.
- This marks the **lowest point in the entire 2022–2024 period**.
- Confirms that the revenue decline was caused by a **massive drop in transaction volume**.

#### 2. Persistent Seasonal Dip in February
- A consistent sharp decline observed **every February** for three consecutive years.
- Order volume consistently drops **below 950,000** before recovering in March.

#### 3. Volatile but Growing Momentum in 2024
- Prior to the November anomaly, 2024 showed **very strong momentum** compared to previous years.
- Q1–Q3 2024 average order volume remained **higher and more stable**, indicating healthy growth in customer base or shopping frequency.

# Product Performance

### Soap Brand Performance

1.	EcoWash demonstrates the most consistent performance among other brands, with average monthly sales exceeding $1,700,000. Unlike other brands, EcoWash rarely experiences sharp declines, making it the most reliable source of revenue.
2.	CleanCo (blue) is the most volatile brand. Although it once fell to a low point in February 2022, the brand managed to reach its highest peak above $2,000,000 in July 2023 and October 2024.
3.	GritOff (yellow) exhibits a unique pattern with sharp spikes every few months, indicating the influence of strong short-term promotions.
4.	A simultaneous decline occurred in February of both 2022 and 2023. This suggests a seasonal pattern where customer purchasing power declines during that month.

---

### Return Trend

1.	Over the past three years, “Defective” has consistently remained the top reason for product returns.
2.	Although the 2024 data is not yet final, this trend indicates that issues with the physical condition of products (defects) are the most urgent problem to address.
3.	There are positive signs in the “Not as Described” category. When compared to 2024, the gap between this category and others is beginning to narrow.
4.	This suggests that the content/marketing team has improved in providing product information, so customers do not feel “misled” when the item arrives.
5.	“Wrong Item” has consistently been the lowest reason for returns every year.
6.	This is evidence that the warehouse operations team performs the most consistently and rarely makes mistakes compared to issues with product quality itself.

# Operational Efficiency

</div>

### Shipping Performance

### Air
Tends to be the most stable mode of transport among the three, with more controlled fluctuations.
It appears to be the highest during certain periods (mid-2022 & early 2023), indicating customer confidence in air shipping for urgent needs.

### Land
It is the most volatile mode, with the most extreme peaks and troughs, dropping to ~300,000 in February 2022 and February 2023.
It often reaches its lowest point at the troughs, but can also reach its highest point at certain peaks.
It shows high sensitivity to changes in demand—likely more influenced by ground operational conditions (weather, infrastructure, etc.).

### Sea
Consistently shows the highest peaks, reaching ~390,000 in July 2022 and September–October 2024.
Sea patterns tend to lag slightly behind Air and Land, which is common due to longer maritime shipping lead times.
It is the preferred mode for high volumes likely because it is more cost-efficient for bulk shipments. 

---

### Discount Impact

A 10% discount generated the highest volume (~9.22 million). This is the optimal point where customers feel they’re getting good value, yet the business can still maintain healthy margins.
The largest discount (20%) actually generated the lowest volume of all. This is a counterintuitive yet crucial finding. Possible reasons include: 
- Quality concerns — A discount that’s too large can trigger the perception that the product is of low quality, defective, or nearing its expiration date.
- Delayed purchase effect — Customers who see a large discount may wait for an even bigger one.
- Different segments — A 20% discount may only attract highly price-sensitive buyers, who generate lower volume.
- Inventory & availability — Products with large discounts are often slow-moving items, which naturally have lower demand.

# Recommendations

</div>

Based on the uncovered insights, here are actionable items that TechSphere can take away from our analysis.

### Sales
Revenue dropped by 20% in November 2024, with order volume plummeting to ~900,000 from a record high of over 1,100,000 in October—the lowest level in three years.
- Conduct an immediate supply chain audit. Determine whether the decline was caused by stockouts, distribution disruptions, or operational failures in November 2024.
- Form a cross-departmental task force (operations, logistics, sales) to develop protocols for early detection of similar anomalies in the future.

A sharp decline occurs every February—order volume consistently drops.
- Plan a special February promotional calendar starting in early January, such as a Valentine’s Day bundle campaign, early-month discounts, or a points-based loyalty program.
- Test early-bird discounts to shift some of March’s demand to February.

AOV remains stable in the range of $8.80–$9.40, but there are extreme fluctuations in May–June each year, as well as a downward trend in October–November 2024.
- Promote upselling and cross-selling in May–June through product bundles or “buy more, save more” promotions to stabilize AOV during this volatile period.
- Evaluate pricing strategies in Q4; the decline in AOV alongside a decrease in order count indicates dual pressures that must be addressed simultaneously.

### Products
EcoWash is the only brand that has demonstrated the most consistent performance among all brands, with stable average monthly sales and no sharp declines.
- Prioritize EcoWash inventory availability across all distribution channels to avoid stockouts. 
- Position EcoWash as the flagship product that supports the company’s overall operational finances.

Both CleanCo and GritOff exhibit unstable sales patterns but have high potential for sales spikes.
- Run short-term promotions for GritOff using retention programs such as loyalty programs or product bundling to encourage customers to make regular purchases.
- Identify the factors driving sales spikes for CleanCo and GritOff, then replicate those strategies during the next peak period.

A simultaneous decline in sales occurred across all brands in February 2022 and 2023, indicating a seasonal pattern that affects consumer purchasing power.
- Design specific marketing campaigns for February, such as Valentine’s Day bundles or early-year hygiene promotions, to stimulate demand.
- Reduce non-essential operational expenses in February to maintain cost efficiency during periods of low demand.

### Return Trend
For three consecutive years, “Defective” has consistently been the primary reason for product returns.
- Strengthen Quality Control (QC) standards before products enter the packaging and distribution process. 
- Establish a defect rate KPI per production batch as a mandatory metric to be routinely monitored by the operations team. 
- Evaluate whether raw materials from suppliers meet established quality standards, especially for products with the highest return rates.
- Conduct a comprehensive audit of the production line to identify which stages most frequently produce defective products.

### Country Performance
Brazil, Canada, France, Germany, and Japan fall within a very narrow performance range (~$33M–$35M), with the total difference between the highest and lowest figures being only about $2 million.
- Select one or two priority countries from this group based on the largest market potential, then focus greater investment there.
- Conduct more aggressive and localized marketing campaigns in those priority countries, tailored to local culture and consumption habits. 
- Set ambitious yet measurable growth targets to drive those selected countries out of the stagnation group and closer to the U.K.’s performance.
